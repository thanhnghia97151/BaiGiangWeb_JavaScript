Wrox Recipes - World's Best Scrambled Eggs

I addapted this recipe from a book called Sydney Food by Bill Grainger. Ever since tasting these eggs on my 1st visit to Bill's restaurant in Kings Cross, Sidney, I have been afther the recipe. I have since transformed it into what I really believe are the best scrambled eggs I have ever tasted.

This recipe is what I called a very special breakfast; just look at the ingredients to see why. It has to be tasted to be believed.

Ingredients

  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon of butter (10g)
  • 1/3 cup of cream (2 3/4 fl ounces)
  • A pinch of salt
  • Freshly milled black pepper
  • 3 fresh chives (chopped)

Instructions

  1. Whisk eggs, cream and salt in a bowl.
  2. Melt the butter in a non-stick pan over a high heat (taking care not to burn the butter)
  3. Pour egg mixture into pan and wait until starts setting around the edge of the pan (around 20 seconds).
  4. Using a wooden spatula, bring the mixture into the center as if it were an omelet, and let it cook for another 20 seconds.
  5. Fold the contents in again, leave for 20 secons and repeat until the eggs are only just done.
  6. Grind a light sprinkling of freshly milled pepper over the eggs and blend in some chopped fresh chives.

You should only make a maximun of two servings per frying pan.
